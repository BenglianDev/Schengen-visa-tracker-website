How to Use Schengen Visa Tracker

1

The Tracker Dashboard

The main Tracker screen shows your current Schengen visa status at a glance. The progress ring displays how many of your 90 days you've used, color-coded green (plenty), orange (running low), or red (critical). Below, you'll see your remaining days and when more days become available as past trips exit the 180-day window.

2

Your Trip List

Scroll down on the Tracker screen to see all your trips. Each card shows the trip name, dates, number of days, and when those days will expire from the 180-day window. Trips are labeled: PLAN for future planned trips, NOW for trips currently in progress. Tap any trip card to jump directly to that month in the Calendar view.

3

Adding a Trip

Tap the blue + button to add trips. Choose "Add a Trip" to record past trips or plan future ones‚Äîjust enter the name and dates. The app automatically categorizes it based on whether the dates are in the past or future. Choose "Quick Trip Calculator" for a quick hypothetical check without saving.

4

Quick Trip Calculator

The Quick Trip Calculator lets you check how a hypothetical trip would affect your visa days‚Äîwithout saving it. Enter your proposed start date and see instantly how many days you can stay. Toggle "Include planned trips" to factor in trips you've already planned. Perfect for checking before you book flights!

5

Calendar Views

The Calendar tab shows your travel history visually. Blue days are past trips, lilac days are planned future trips. Each day displays your remaining Schengen days for that date. Toggle between Month and Year views. In Year view, tap any month to zoom in. The legend shows your total past and planned days.

6

Swipe to Edit or Delete

On the Tracker screen, swipe left on any trip card to edit it, or swipe right to delete it. Visual indicators appear as you swipe‚Äîblue for edit, red for delete. This makes managing your trips quick and intuitive without cluttering the interface with buttons.

7

Information & FAQ

The Info tab contains frequently asked questions about Schengen travel rules. Learn which countries are in the Schengen Area, how microstates and overseas territories work, who can travel visa-free, how days are counted, and information about the upcoming ETIAS system. Everything you need to understand the 90/180 rule.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 25, 2024

Data Collection

Schengen Visa Tracker does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers. All trip data is stored locally on your device only. We believe your travel history is personal information that should remain private.

Data Storage

Your travel records are saved using your device's local storage. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third parties. Even we cannot see your trips or travel patterns.

Third-Party Services

This app does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. We don't use Firebase, Google Analytics, or any other tracking tools. The only external service used is Google Play for processing purchases, which is handled entirely by Google according to their privacy policy.

In-App Purchases

Purchases are processed through Google Play. We do not have access to your payment information. Google handles all billing securely according to their own privacy and security standards.

Data Deletion

You can delete all your data at any time by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's data in your device settings. Since no data is stored on our servers, uninstalling the app permanently removes all your information.

Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy occasionally. Any changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
